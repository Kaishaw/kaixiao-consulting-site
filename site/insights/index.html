Industry notes

Industry notes on CRM, Power Platform, and AI.

A short, practical view of what is changing in customer operations and what still needs good process design.

01

AI in CRM is only useful when the business process is clear.

Copilot-style features are becoming part of sales and service tools, but AI cannot fix unclear ownership, duplicated customer data, or messy escalation rules. My view: before asking AI to summarize, route, or recommend, teams need a clean operating model and enough trust in the CRM data.

02

Contact centers are moving closer to CRM and knowledge management.

Microsoft positions Dynamics 365 Contact Center as Copilot-first, which fits a bigger shift: service work is no longer only about answering cases. It is about connecting case history, customer profile, knowledge, channels, and follow-up actions in one flow.

03

Power Platform is becoming the business team's extension layer.

Low-code tools are useful when they sit close to a real business pain: approvals, handoffs, simple apps, reporting routines, and manual checks. The risk is building too many disconnected apps. Governance and simple design standards matter.

04

The best functional consultants translate between people.

A lot of value comes from translation: business users explain pain points, developers need exact behavior, testers need scenarios, and leaders need confidence. A good BA or functional consultant makes that translation visible and testable.
